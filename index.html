End of the Year Contest

The first edition of the End of the Year Contest will be held in a couple of weeks. The challenge is to code a bot for a computer game within a limited amount of time, i.e. to program an AI. You can work in teams of 3-4 people on one (provided) laptop, without internet access. The game you will code a bot for will be revealed on the day itself. Afterwards there's a free (!) barbecue and during which the tournament will take place. This is whereyour bot will compete against the other teams. There will also be a grand prize for the team that places first.

Even if you are not much of a programmer yourself, you can still compete. We will also provide you with code in order to help you get started. Not able to find a complete team? Register as a partial team and we will see what we can do for you.

Interested? Read on for the practical details!

Practical

The contest will take place on Tuesday, June 21. Note that this is during the exam weeks, but it can also be a nice distraction from exam learning.

The contest is free of charge and there will be a free barbecue provided at the end.

The contest will take place in the Flux building and the barbecue is held in front of MetaForum.

Organization

This contest is organized by a group of Honors students from the 'Competitive Programming and Problem Solving' track. We work closely together with the Honors Study Association Confluente.
